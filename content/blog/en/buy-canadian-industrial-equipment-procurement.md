## Why Buy Canadian Industrial Equipment?

Canadian-made industrial equipment eliminates ITAR export restrictions, reduces supply chain risk, qualifies for federal procurement preferences, and provides same-country warranty service. Since 2022, Canadian manufacturers have faced 12-18 week lead times on imported vibration equipment and laser alignment systems — compared to 2-4 weeks from domestic manufacturers. The 2024-2025 trade disruptions increased this gap further. Buy-Canadian procurement saves 15-25% on total cost of ownership when you factor in shipping, duties, lead times, currency risk, and local support.

## Five Economic Arguments

**1. No ITAR restrictions.** US-manufactured precision instruments often fall under International Traffic in Arms Regulations. Canadian-made equivalents ship freely to any country without export licences, end-use certificates, or compliance paperwork. For Ontario companies serving international clients, this eliminates weeks of procurement delay.

**2. Supply chain resilience.** The 2024-2025 supply chain disruptions hit imported equipment hard. Canadian manufacturers with domestic supply chains maintained 2-4 week lead times while imported equipment stretched to 16+ weeks. Production lost waiting for equipment dwarfs any price difference.

**3. Federal procurement preferences.** The Canadian Content Policy and the Buy Clean strategy give Canadian-made products a competitive advantage in government procurement. Indigenous procurement requirements (PSAB) can be met more easily with Canadian manufacturers.

**4. Currency protection.** Buying in CAD eliminates USD/CAD exchange risk. With the Canadian dollar fluctuating 10-15% against the USD in recent years, a $50,000 USD purchase can cost anywhere from CAD $62,000 to CAD $72,000 depending on timing.

**5. Local warranty and support.** A sensor that fails on a Saturday night in Hamilton gets replaced by Monday morning when the manufacturer is in Burlington. The same failure with an imported product means a warranty claim, international shipping, and 2-3 weeks without the instrument.

### Is Canadian-made equipment as good as imported brands?

Yes. Canadian vibration equipment, laser alignment systems, and thermography instruments meet the same ISO and CSA standards as imported equivalents. Our instruments are calibrated in our own ISO 17025-accredited lab in Burlington, Ontario. Performance specifications match or exceed major international brands.

### Does buying Canadian qualify for SR&ED tax credits?

The purchase of equipment itself does not qualify for SR&ED. However, if you use Canadian equipment in SR&ED-eligible R&D activities, the equipment costs may be claimable as capital expenditures under the 2026 SR&ED rules.
