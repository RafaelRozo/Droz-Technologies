§ Discipline · 03

Ultrasound — caught before vibration.

Acoustic detection of early-stage faults. Lubrication regime, leak detection, electrical partial discharge, and bearing pre-failure regimes — captured before vibration onset and before the spectrum has the signature.

§ What it detects

Acoustic signatures we identify.

DETECTS · 03

Lubrication, leaks, partial discharge, bearings

  • Lubrication regime — starvation, over-lubrication, and contaminated grease detected acoustically before the bearing's vibration signature appears. The earliest, lowest-cost intervention in the program.
  • Pneumatic, gas, and steam leaks — compressed-air leakage is a multi-million-dollar energy loss at scale; ultrasound localizes leaks across plant utility lines and process piping under operating pressure.
  • Electrical partial discharge — corona, tracking, and arcing in MV/HV switchgear and transformers captured acoustically before thermal signature develops.
  • Early-stage bearing faults — high-frequency ultrasonic emissions from incipient bearing damage precede the FFT spectrum by weeks; the discipline's defining advantage.
§ Methodology

Three approaches, one acoustic surface.

METHOD · 01

Airborne
ultrasound

Non-contact probe captures airborne emissions — pneumatic and steam leaks, corona discharge, valve passing — at distances suited to safe stand-off from energized equipment.

METHOD · 02

Structure-borne
(contact)

Contact probe on bearing housings and gearbox cases captures structure-borne high-frequency emissions — the earliest bearing-fault signal, well ahead of vibration onset.

Combined ultrasonic-listening and grease-replenishment route — operators hear the bearing accept the right quantity of grease, eliminating over- and under-lubrication.

§ Reporting cadence

Ultrasound routes typically piggyback the vibration route on rotating equipment — same cadence, same engineer, complementary signal. Lubrication-listening surveys run on a calibrated cadence per asset population (often monthly for production-critical assets). Compressed-air and steam-leak surveys run annually for utility cost recovery; partial-discharge surveys on MV/HV switchgear align with the thermography cadence.
