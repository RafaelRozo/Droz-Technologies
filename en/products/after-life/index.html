§ What it is

Built during life. Continued, on the principal's terms.

After Life is a digital-persona platform. During life, a person and their trustee define what should be built, what may continue, and on whose terms. The platform learns from the person's voice, their writing, the memories they choose to record, and the platforms they use. After death — or at the person's own instruction — the persona continues that presence under the rules its principal set, and under the supervision of the trustee.

  • Voice and likeness learning during life. The person participates in building their own persona; nothing is reconstructed without them.
  • Conversational presence for family members. Text and voice conversations with the persona, available privately to the family.
  • Continued posting and scheduled communications. Pre-authored messages on the platforms the principal chose, only where the principal authorized.
  • Privacy and consent at the center. Nothing happens that the principal did not explicitly authorize. The trustee enforces.

Trustees, family-office operators, and executors who carry the responsibility for what runs and what doesn't, after a principal's passing.

§ Ethics & consent

The ethical framework is the architecture.

After Life is a serious product about a serious subject. The ethical framework is not bolted on — it is the architecture.

  • — The living person controls what is built and what may run.
  • — A trustee enforces the rules.
  • — The platform respects right-to-delete.
  • — Posthumous use only with documented consent.
  • — No commercial use without explicit authorization.
  • — Transparent labeling on every interaction — "you are speaking with a model of [name]".
§ Compliance & jurisdiction

Aligned with the regimes that exist — evolving with the ones that come next.

The legal framework for digital personas is forming in real time. After Life is built to align with the regimes that exist today and to evolve with the ones that come next.

  • PIPEDA — Canada.
  • RUFADAA — Revised Uniform Fiduciary Access to Digital Assets Act, adopted in nearly every US state.
  • GDPR — European Union.
  • Quebec Law 25 — Quebec.
  • State / province by jurisdiction — where digital-asset legislation applies.
